### Question 1: 
A cannonball is shot from ground level at an angle of 37-degrees above a level field. The initial velocity of the cannonball has a magnitude of 50 m/s. Neglect air resistance and answer the following questions:

a) Find the horizontal and vertical components of the initial velocity vector.

> (Hint: Draw the initial velocity and use some trig to break it into its horizontal and vertical components.)

b) How many seconds does the cannonball stay in the air?

> (Hint: What is the vertical position of the ball when it lands on the level ground? You should be able to use this knowledge to find the time.)

c) How far from the cannon will the ball land?

> (Hint: The time you found in part (b) will be helpful here.)

d) How high above the field is the cannonball at its highest point?

> (Hint: You could find the time for this point using something we know about vertical velocity at the maximum height. That time could then be used to get the vertical position. )
