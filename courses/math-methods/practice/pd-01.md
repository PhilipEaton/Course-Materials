9) Just because this is a practice day doesn't mean we can't learn something new using the tools we already have! A **projection matrix** is a square ($ n \times n $) matrix $ \mathbf{P} $ that satisfies the property $ \mathbf{P}^2 = \mathbf{P} $. This property means that applying the projection multiple times does not change the result. Projection matrices are used to map vectors onto things like lines or planes, more generally called subspaces.

*Here is the idea*: In general, a mathematical operator acts on objects position to their right. Our goal is to construct an operator that calculates how much of a vector $ \vec{v} $ lies in the direction of another vector $ \vec{u} $ and then points the result in the direction of $\vec{u}$. This can be achieved using dot products.

First, we note that a unit vector $ \hat{u} $ in the direction of $ \vec{u} $ can be written as  

$$
\hat{u} = \frac{\vec{u}}{|\vec{u}|} = \frac{\vec{u}}{\sqrt{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u}}}
$$  

Using this, we can define the projection of $ \vec{v} $ onto $ \vec{u} $ in the following manner

$$
\begin{aligned}
    \text{Projection of $\vec{v}$ onto $\vec{u}$} &= \text{Proj}_{\vec{u}} \vec{v} \\[0.75ex]
    &= (\, \hat{u}\cdot\vec{v} \, ) \, \hat{u}  \\[0.75ex]
    &= \left(\, \left(\frac{\vec{u}}{\sqrt{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{u}}}\right)\cdot\vec{v} \, \right) \, \left(\frac{\vec{u}}{\sqrt{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{u}}}\right)  \\[0.75ex]
    &= \left(\, \frac{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}}{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{u}}\, \right) \, \vec{u}  \\[0.75ex]
    &= \underbrace{\left(\frac{\vec{u}^\text{T} \,  \vec{v}}{\vec{u}^\text{T} \, \vec{u}} \right)}_\text{A scalar} \,\,\,  \vec{u}   \\[0.75ex]
    &= \vec{u} \,\, \left(\frac{\vec{u}^\text{T} \,  \vec{v}}{\vec{u}^\text{T} \, \vec{u}} \right)   \\[0.75ex]
    &= \vec{u} \,\, \left(\frac{\vec{u}^\text{T} \,}{\vec{u}^\text{T} \, \vec{u}} \,\,\, \vec{v} \right)   \\[0.75ex]
    &= \underbrace{\left(\vec{u} \,\, \frac{\vec{u}^\text{T} \,}{\vec{u}^\text{T} \, \vec{u}}\right)}_\text{An Operator}  \,\,\, \vec{v} 
\end{aligned}
$$

The corresponding projection matrix $ \mathbf{P}_{\vec{u}} $ that acts on any vector to produce its projection onto the vector $\vec{u}$ is  

$$
\mathbf{P}_{\vec{u}} = \vec{u}  \,\,\, \frac{\vec{u}^\text{T}}{\vec{u}^\text{T} \vec{u}}
$$  

where $ \vec{u} $ is a column vector.

Compute the projection of the vector  

$$
\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}
$$  

onto the vector  

$$
\vec{u} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}
$$  

Then, construct the projection matrix $ \mathbf{P} $ that performs this operation.
