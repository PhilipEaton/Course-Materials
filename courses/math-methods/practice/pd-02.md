<!-- ############### -->
8) For a system of two coupled masses with a spring constant $ k $, the mass and stiffness matrices are given by:

$$
\mathbf{M} = \begin{bmatrix} m & 0 \\ 0 & m \end{bmatrix} \qquad
\mathbf{K} = \begin{bmatrix} 2k & -k \\ -k & 2k \end{bmatrix}
$$

Recall for a spring-mass oscillator, the angular frequency of oscillation is given by $ \omega^2 = \frac{k}{m} $. In the matrix formulation, the relationship between the angular frequencies and the system dynamics is captured by:

$$
\boldsymbol{\omega} = \mathbf{M}^{-1} \mathbf{K}.
$$

The matrix $ \boldsymbol{\omega} $ contains information about the angular frequencies of the coupled vibrations in the system. Specifically, the eigenvalues of $ \boldsymbol{\omega} $ correspond to the squared angular frequencies ($ \omega^2 $) of the normal modes of vibration. Calculate the eigenvalues of $ \boldsymbol{\omega} = \mathbf{M}^{-1} \mathbf{K} $ and determine the angular frequencies of the system.
